Our research is focused on addressing problems in biomedical research using micro-/nano-technology. We are particularly interested in

  • Microfluidic chips for in vitro diagnostics
  • Digital nucleic acid tests
  • Single cell analysis based on droplet microfluidics

Some of the past and ongoing projects are shown below. Available facilities are shown here.

Microfluidic chips for in vitro diagnostics

Conventional laboratory tests happen in a central lab with high cost and high turn-around time. Microfluidics offers a way to develop point-of-care testings with reduced cost and waiting time chip for biomarker monitoring at bedside, on emergency scenes, and at home. Leveraging our expertise in microfabrication and fluid mechanics, we aim to develop microfluidic chips with real-life impacts.

Digital nucleic acid tests

qPCR requires references to quantify the concentration of nucleic acids. In contrast, digital PCR offers a means for the absolute quantification without the need of references. However, existing digital PCR methods require sophisticated instruments for compartmentalization and signal reading, limiting their widespread applications. We aim to develop low cost, portable methods for digital nucleic acid tests.

Single cell analysis based on droplet microfluidics

Bulk analysis takes ensemble average of the biological samples, while single cell analysis offers the opportunity to tease out cell heterogeneity. A typical pipeline of single cell analysis including cell isolation, library construction, sequencing, and stats, and droplet microfluidics offers a method to isolate and process cells with ultrahigh throughput. We are currently working on improving the performance of droplet microfluidics in single cell analysis, in terms of cell encapsulation, droplet sorting, and barcoding. We work closely with our collaborators in sequencing industry.
